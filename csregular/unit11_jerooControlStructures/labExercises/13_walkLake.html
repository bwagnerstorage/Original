Recursion

Recursion is a programming technique where a method calls itself. Recursion is useful because it allows your program to repeat a process just like the while control structure. Let's look at an example. Suppose I have a Jeroo named bob located on the west shore of an island and I want him to walk across the island until he reaches the east shore. Using recursion I could do the following.

In the main method create the Jeroo bob and place him on the west shore of the island then call the user defined method walk.




In the walk method have bob hop forward one space then have the method call itself. This call to walk method is a recursive call because we are making a call to method that we are currently in. When the program reaches the call to walk method it returns execution back to the top of the walk method and starts over again.




There is, however, one flaw in this program. The Jeroo does not stop when it reaches the east shore. When you use recursion it is important to add a condition to make the recursion stop. In programming terminology we call this a base case. Look at the modified walk method.




I have added the condition that the recursion will only continue if the water is not ahead. Now the Jeroo will stop when it reaches the east shore.
